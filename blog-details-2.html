The quality and relevance of data significantly impact the insights you can derive. Identify and gather data from relevant sources, including internal databases, third-party sources, IoT devices, social media, and more.
  • Raw data often contains errors, inconsistencies, and missing values that can hinder analysis. Before proceeding with analysis, perform data cleaning and preprocessing tasks to ensure data quality and consistency. This may involve tasks such as removing duplicates, handling missing values, standardizing formats, and correcting errors.
  • EDA is a crucial step in uncovering initial insights and patterns within your data. Utilize descriptive statistics, data visualization techniques, and exploratory visualizations to gain a preliminary understanding of the data distribution, relationships between variables, and potential outliers or anomalies.
  • Data analysis is often an iterative process that involves refining your approach based on initial findings.
